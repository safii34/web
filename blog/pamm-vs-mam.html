PAMM vs MAM Accounts

PAMM and MAM are two widely used investment solutions in Forex account management.

What is a PAMM Account?

PAMM (Percentage Allocation Management Module) allows investors to allocate funds to a professional trader. Profits and losses are distributed proportionally according to each investor's share.

What is a MAM Account?

MAM (Multi Account Manager) enables professional traders to manage multiple client accounts simultaneously while allowing flexible trade allocation.

Main Differences

  • PAMM uses percentage-based allocation.
  • MAM offers more flexibility for individual accounts.
  • MAM is often preferred by professional account managers.
  • PAMM is generally simpler for investors.

Which One is Better?

The best choice depends on investment goals, risk tolerance and account management requirements.
